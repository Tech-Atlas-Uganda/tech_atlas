## ğŸ—³ï¸ Decision-Making Process

### Decision Types

| Decision Type | Who Decides | Process | Timeline |
|---------------|-------------|---------|----------|
| ğŸ”§ **Minor Technical** | Core Contributors | Consensus | 3-7 days |
| ğŸ¨ **Design Changes** | Core Contributors + Moderators | Consensus | 5-10 days |
| ğŸ“ **Content Policy** | Moderators + Steering Committee | Vote | 7-14 days |
| ğŸš€ **Major Features** | Steering Committee + Community Input | Vote | 14-30 days |
| ğŸ›ï¸ **Governance Changes** | Steering Committee + Community Vote | Supermajority | 30+ days |

### Decision-Making Principles

#### 1. **Consensus First**
We strive for consensus on all decisions. Disagreements are resolved through discussion and compromise.

#### 2. **Transparency**
All decisions and their rationale are documented and shared with the community.

#### 3. **Community Input**
Major decisions include a community feedback period before finalization.

#### 4. **Reversibility**
Most decisions can be revisited if new information emerges or community needs change.

### Proposal Process

#### Step 1: **Proposal Submission**
- Create GitHub Discussion or Issue
- Use proposal template
- Clearly describe problem and solution
- Tag relevant stakeholders

#### Step 2: **Community Feedback** (7-14 days)
- Community members comment and discuss
- Proposer addresses questions and concerns
- Proposal may be refined based on feedback

#### Step 3: **Review** (3-7 days)
- Relevant governance tier reviews proposal
- Technical feasibility assessed
- Resource requirements evaluated

#### Step 4: **Decision**
- Consensus: Proposal approved
- Vote: If consensus not reached
- Rejection: With clear rationale

#### Step 5: **Implementation**
- Approved proposals added to roadmap
- Contributors assigned
- Progress tracked publicly
