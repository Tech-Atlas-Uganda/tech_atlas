## Problem Statement

### The Challenge

Uganda's technology ecosystem faces a critical **information fragmentation problem**:

1. **Scattered Information**: Data about hubs, communities, startups, jobs, and opportunities is dispersed across:
   - Personal WhatsApp groups
   - Facebook pages
   - Twitter threads
   - Individual websites
   - Word-of-mouth networks

2. **Discovery Barriers**: New entrants struggle to:
   - Find relevant communities and hubs
   - Discover job opportunities
   - Access learning resources
   - Learn about events and opportunities
   - Connect with other professionals

3. **Network Effects**: Opportunities often circulate within closed networks, creating:
   - Unequal access to information
   - Missed opportunities for talented individuals
   - Slower ecosystem growth
   - Reduced collaboration potential

4. **Duplication of Effort**: Multiple organizations maintain separate, incomplete directories

5. **No Single Source of Truth**: No authoritative, up-to-date resource for ecosystem information

### The Impact

This fragmentation results in:
- **Talent Loss**: Skilled professionals leave due to lack of visible opportunities
- **Slow Growth**: Startups struggle to find talent, funding, and resources
- **Reduced Collaboration**: Communities operate in silos
- **Missed Opportunities**: Events, grants, and jobs go unnoticed
- **Inefficient Resource Allocation**: Duplicated efforts and wasted resources
